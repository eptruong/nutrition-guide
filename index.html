Chapter 1

üß† Brain & Cognition

Your brain is the most metabolically expensive organ ‚Äî 2% of body weight, 20% of energy. What you eat directly determines how well it runs.

Brain Fog HIGH confidence

Brain fog is a real symptom cluster ‚Äî impaired concentration, poor recall, mental sluggishness, difficulty finding words. It's not a diagnosis; it's a downstream signal that something upstream is broken.

  • Neuroinflammation ‚Äî pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-Œ±) cross the blood-brain barrier, activate microglia, slow synaptic transmission
  • Gut-brain axis ‚Äî dysbiotic bacteria produce LPS (lipopolysaccharide) ‚Üí enters bloodstream via leaky gut ‚Üí triggers systemic + neuroinflammation
  • DHA deficiency ‚Äî brain is ~60% fat by dry weight, primarily DHA. Low DHA = impaired membrane fluidity = slower signal propagation
  • B12 deficiency ‚Äî B12 is essential for myelin sheath synthesis. Demyelination = signal leakage, cognitive decline
  • Blood sugar instability ‚Äî neurons rely on glucose; reactive hypoglycemia (sugar crash) starves them
  • Poor sleep ‚Äî the glymphatic system clears Œ≤-amyloid and metabolic waste only during deep sleep
Supporting Evidence

Gut-Brain Axis & Neuroinflammation

A 2019 systematic review in Frontiers in Neuroscience found that gut microbiome dysbiosis is consistently associated with neuroinflammatory conditions including cognitive impairment, depression, and anxiety. LPS from gram-negative bacteria activates TLR4 receptors on microglia.

Cryan et al. (2019). "The Microbiota-Gut-Brain Axis." Physiological Reviews. doi:10.1152/physrev.00018.2018
Opposing View

"Brain fog isn't a real condition"

Some clinicians dismiss brain fog as vague and non-diagnostic. They're technically right ‚Äî it's not in the DSM or ICD. But that's a classification issue, not a biological one. The underlying mechanisms (neuroinflammation, nutrient deficiency, metabolic dysfunction) are well-documented. The symptom is real; the medical system just doesn't have a code for it.

DHA & Brain Structure

Why Your Brain Needs Fat HIGH confidence

DHA (docosahexaenoic acid) is the primary structural omega-3 in the brain and retina. It constitutes 40% of polyunsaturated fatty acids in the brain and 60% in the retina's photoreceptor outer segments.

  • Membrane fluidity ‚Äî DHA's 6 double bonds create a uniquely flexible molecule, keeping cell membranes fluid so receptors and ion channels function properly
  • Synaptogenesis ‚Äî DHA promotes new synapse formation and dendritic branching
  • Neuroprotectin D1 ‚Äî DHA metabolizes into NPD1, a potent anti-inflammatory mediator that protects neurons from oxidative stress
  • BDNF production ‚Äî DHA increases brain-derived neurotrophic factor, critical for learning and memory
Bazinet & Lay√© (2014). "Polyunsaturated fatty acids and their metabolites in brain function and disease." Nature Reviews Neuroscience. doi:10.1038/nrn3820

B12 & Myelin

The Insulation Layer HIGH confidence

Vitamin B12 (cobalamin) is required for methylation reactions that produce the myelin sheath ‚Äî the fatty insulation around nerve fibers. Without adequate B12, myelin degrades, signals leak, and nerve conduction slows. This is why B12 deficiency causes numbness, cognitive decline, and eventually irreversible neurological damage.

  • B12 is a cofactor for methionine synthase ‚Üí produces SAMe ‚Üí methylation of myelin basic protein
  • Deficiency signs: fatigue, brain fog, tingling in extremities, balance problems
  • Vegans and elderly are most at risk (no animal B12, declining intrinsic factor)
  • Serum B12 can be "normal" while tissue levels are low ‚Äî methylmalonic acid (MMA) is a better marker

Sleep & the Glymphatic System

Your Brain's Waste Disposal HIGH confidence

The glymphatic system ‚Äî discovered in 2012 ‚Äî is the brain's dedicated waste clearance pathway. During deep sleep, brain cells shrink by ~60%, allowing cerebrospinal fluid to flush through interstitial spaces and clear metabolic waste including Œ≤-amyloid (Alzheimer's plaques) and tau proteins.

  • Active primarily during deep (N3) sleep ‚Äî reduced by 90% during wakefulness
  • Lateral sleeping position enhances clearance vs supine or prone
  • Chronic sleep deprivation ‚Üí waste accumulation ‚Üí neurodegeneration
  • Alcohol impairs glymphatic function even if total sleep time seems adequate
Xie et al. (2013). "Sleep drives metabolite clearance from the adult brain." Science. doi:10.1126/science.1241224
üî¨ Honest Take: Brain fog is one of the best-supported symptom clusters in functional medicine. The mechanisms (neuroinflammation, DHA deficiency, B12 depletion, gut-brain signaling) are individually well-researched. What's less clear is the relative contribution of each factor for any given person ‚Äî it's usually multifactorial. Fix the gut, fix the sleep, fix the nutrients, and most brain fog resolves.

The Fiber Paradox

When Fiber Works

Healthy Microbiome ‚úì

  • Correct bacteria present (Roseburia, Faecalibacterium)
  • Butyrate produced efficiently
  • Gut barrier strengthened
  • Regular, easy bowel movements
  • Gas production minimal and well-tolerated
When Fiber Hurts

Dysbiotic Gut ‚úó

  • Wrong bacteria ferment fiber ‚Üí excess gas, bloating
  • Can worsen SIBO (small intestinal bacterial overgrowth)
  • Insoluble fiber adds bulk ‚Üí harder stools if motility is impaired
  • Feeds pathogenic bacteria alongside beneficial ones
  • Phytates and oxalates in high-fiber foods block mineral absorption
Key Study

Stopping Fiber Resolved Constipation MEDIUM confidence

A 2012 study by Ho et al. placed 63 constipated patients on a zero-fiber diet for 2 weeks, then allowed them to choose their fiber intake. Those who stayed on zero fiber had daily bowel movements (vs once every 6-7 days on high fiber). Bloating and pain also resolved completely.

Ho et al. (2012). "Stopping or reducing dietary fiber intake reduces constipation and its associated symptoms." World J Gastroenterol. doi:10.3748/wjg.v18.i33.4593
Opposing View

"Fiber is universally beneficial" ‚Äî Most dietary guidelines

Large observational studies (Nurses' Health Study, EPIC) associate higher fiber intake with lower all-cause mortality, colorectal cancer risk, and cardiovascular disease. Why this may be misleading:

  • Healthy user bias ‚Äî people who eat fiber also exercise more, smoke less, drink less alcohol, eat less processed food
  • Confounders ‚Äî high fiber intake correlates with overall dietary quality, not just fiber itself
  • Observational ‚â† causal ‚Äî these studies can't isolate fiber as the active variable
  • Ignores individual microbiome state ‚Äî what works for a healthy gut may harm a dysbiotic one
‚ö° Reality: Fiber is a prebiotic ‚Äî food for bacteria. If your bacteria are good, fiber helps. If they're bad, fiber makes everything worse. The mainstream "eat more fiber" advice skips the prerequisite: you need a functioning microbiome first. Raw dairy (kefir especially) supplies the bacteria directly ‚Äî no fermentation gamble required.

Raw Dairy as Microbiome Builder

Direct Inoculation vs Prebiotic Feeding MEDIUM confidence

  • Raw milk / kefir ‚Äî contains live Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Leuconostoc + 100+ other species
  • Directly populates the gut with beneficial bacteria ‚Äî no fermentation dependency
  • Also supplies: butyrate (in butterfat directly), immunoglobulins (IgA), lactoferrin (antimicrobial)
  • Pasteurization kills all this ‚Äî pasteurized milk is nutritionally different from raw milk
  • Raw butter contains butyrate directly (3-4% by weight) ‚Äî no microbial fermentation needed
Opposing View

"Raw milk is dangerous" ‚Äî FDA, CDC

Regulators warn of Salmonella, E. coli O157:H7, Listeria, and Campylobacter contamination. Nuance:

  • Risk is real but context matters ‚Äî properly handled raw milk from grass-fed, tested herds has an excellent safety record
  • A 2015 study found 0 confirmed outbreaks from legal raw milk dairies with testing protocols (most outbreaks traced to improper handling or illegal sales)
  • Raw milk contains its own antimicrobial system (lactoferrin, lactoperoxidase, IgA) that actively suppresses pathogenic bacteria ‚Äî pasteurized milk lacks this self-protection
  • The dose-response matters: industrial CAFO milk is genuinely dangerous raw; small-farm grass-fed with testing is a different product

Leaky Gut (Intestinal Permeability)

The Barrier Breakdown HIGH confidence

Your intestinal lining is one cell thick. Tight junction proteins (claudins, occludin, ZO-1) seal the gaps between enterocytes. When these junctions loosen:

  • Undigested food proteins enter the bloodstream ‚Üí immune system attacks them ‚Üí food sensitivities
  • Bacterial LPS enters circulation ‚Üí TLR4 activation ‚Üí systemic inflammation
  • Triggers: gluten (zonulin release), NSAIDs, alcohol, chronic stress, dysbiosis
  • Measurable via lactulose/mannitol test or serum zonulin
Fasano (2012). "Leaky Gut and Autoimmune Diseases." Clinical Reviews in Allergy & Immunology. doi:10.1007/s12016-011-8291-x
üî¨ Honest Take: Butyrate's role as colonocyte fuel and gut barrier maintainer is rock-solid science. The fiber paradox is real but under-studied ‚Äî the Ho 2012 study is small (n=63) and unblinded. Larger RCTs are needed but unlikely to happen (no one profits from "eat less fiber"). The raw dairy microbiome evidence is promising but largely observational and from small studies. Confidence is high on mechanisms, medium on optimal strategies.

Key Principles

Competition

Nutrients sharing the same transporter compete for binding sites. Taking high-dose zinc? You're depleting copper. Mega-dosing iron? You're blocking zinc. This is why single-nutrient supplements can create imbalances that whole foods don't.

  • Zinc vs Copper ‚Äî zinc induces metallothionein, which traps copper ‚Üí copper deficiency at >50mg/day zinc
  • Iron vs Zinc ‚Äî both use DMT1 ‚Üí co-supplementation reduces absorption of both
  • Calcium vs Magnesium ‚Äî high calcium intake reduces Mg absorption at the intestinal level
  • Calcium vs Iron ‚Äî calcium inhibits both heme and non-heme iron absorption when taken together

Saturation HIGH confidence

Transporters have a maximum capacity. Beyond that, nutrients are excreted or simply not absorbed:

  • Vitamin C ‚Äî SVCT1 saturates at ~200mg. A 1000mg supplement = ~200mg absorbed + 800mg in the toilet
  • B12 ‚Äî only ~1.5mcg absorbed per dose via intrinsic factor + ~1% passive diffusion for amounts above that
  • Calcium ‚Äî absorption efficiency drops from ~45% at 200mg to ~15% at 1500mg per dose

PUFA Oxidation ‚Äî The Real Danger

Why Seed Oils Are Toxic

Lipid Peroxidation Chemistry HIGH confidence

PUFAs have multiple double bonds ‚Äî each is a site vulnerable to oxidative attack. When exposed to heat, light, or oxygen:

  • Step 1: Free radical abstracts hydrogen from double bond ‚Üí lipid radical formed
  • Step 2: Lipid radical reacts with O‚ÇÇ ‚Üí lipid peroxyl radical ‚Üí chain reaction begins
  • Step 3: Produces toxic aldehydes: 4-HNE (4-hydroxynonenal), MDA (malondialdehyde)
  • Step 4: Aldehydes damage DNA (mutagenic), cross-link proteins, destroy cell membranes
  • Step 5: Forms ALEs (advanced lipoxidation end-products) ‚Üí accelerated aging
  • Step 6: Oxidized LDL ‚Üí foam cells ‚Üí atherosclerotic plaques

Seed oils (canola, soy, sunflower, corn) are 50-75% PUFA, extracted with hexane solvent at high temperatures, deodorized (more heat) ‚Äî they're already oxidized before you buy them.

Esterbauer et al. (1991). "Chemistry and biochemistry of 4-hydroxynonenal, malonaldehyde and related aldehydes." Free Radical Biology and Medicine. doi:10.1016/0891-5849(91)90192-6

AGEs (Advanced Glycation End-Products) MEDIUM confidence

  • What: Proteins or lipids glycated (bonded with sugars) non-enzymatically ‚Äî accelerated by high heat
  • Where: Form during grilling, roasting, frying. Highest in browned/charred food
  • Damage: Cross-link collagen (stiff joints, wrinkled skin), activate RAGE receptors (inflammation), impair blood vessel elasticity

  • Minimization: Cook at lower temperatures, shorter times, with moisture (stewing > grilling). Rare > well-done.
Opposing View

"The anti-seed-oil movement is unscientific"

Some researchers argue that linoleic acid (LA) has been unfairly demonized, pointing to RCTs where replacing saturated fat with LA reduced LDL cholesterol. Why this is incomplete:

  • LDL reduction ‚â† cardiovascular benefit. The Sydney Diet Heart Study and Minnesota Coronary Experiment both showed LA replacement lowered LDL but increased mortality
  • These trials used fresh, carefully handled oils ‚Äî not the repeatedly heated, hexane-extracted oils consumed in real life
  • The oxidation question is separate from the total-LA question. Even if LA at controlled intake isn't harmful, oxidized LA definitely is
  • Tissue accumulation of LA takes 2+ years to clear ‚Äî short-term trials miss long-term effects
Ramsden et al. (2016). "Re-evaluation of the traditional diet-heart hypothesis." BMJ. doi:10.1136/bmj.e8707

üíä Vitamins Deep Dive

Not all vitamins are created equal. The form, source, and context determine whether they help or harm.

Vitamin D: D2 vs D3

D2 ‚Äî Ergocalciferol

Plant/Fungal Form

  • Found in UV-exposed mushrooms, fortified foods
  • Does convert to 25(OH)D in liver ‚Äî but less efficiently
  • Shorter half-life (~15 days vs ~25 days for D3)
  • 87% less potent at raising blood levels per unit
  • May actually decrease D3 levels when supplemented
  • Not what your body naturally produces
D3 ‚Äî Cholecalciferol

Animal/Sun Form

  • Made by your skin from UVB + 7-dehydrocholesterol
  • Found in fatty fish, egg yolks, cod liver oil
  • 87% more effective at raising serum 25(OH)D
  • Longer half-life, better tissue distribution
  • Stored in adipose tissue for winter use
  • The form your body evolved with

Vitamin D Synthesis Pathway HIGH confidence

UVB hits skin ‚Üí 7-DHC ‚Üí Pre-D3 ‚Üí D3 (cholecalciferol) ‚Üí Liver: 25(OH)D ‚Üí Kidney: 1,25(OH)‚ÇÇD [active]

D2 follows a similar pathway but produces 25(OH)D2, which the body handles less efficiently. D2 may also compete with D3 for 25-hydroxylase binding, potentially reducing D3 activation.

Heaney et al. (2011). "Vitamin D3 is more potent than vitamin D2 in humans." J Clin Endocrinol Metab. doi:10.1210/jc.2010-2230

Fat-Soluble Vitamins (A, D, E, K)

All Need Dietary Fat HIGH confidence

Fat-soluble vitamins are absorbed through the same pathway as dietary fat:

Bile emulsifies fat ‚Üí Micelle formation ‚Üí Vitamins dissolve in micelles ‚Üí Absorbed into enterocytes ‚Üí Packaged into chylomicrons ‚Üí Enter lymphatic system ‚Üí blood

Without adequate dietary fat (or with bile insufficiency), you can eat all the vitamins you want and they'll pass right through you. Low-fat diets are a recipe for fat-soluble vitamin deficiency.

Vitamin K2 ‚Äî The Calcium Director MEDIUM confidence

  • K2 activates osteocalcin (puts calcium INTO bones) and matrix GLA protein (keeps calcium OUT OF arteries)
  • Without K2: calcium supplementation can deposit in arteries instead of bones ‚Äî calcification/atherosclerosis
  • MK-4 (animal form) ‚Äî rapidly absorbed, short half-life, found in grass-fed butter/organs. Converts from K1 in some tissues
  • MK-7 (bacterial form) ‚Äî longer half-life, found in natto, aged cheese. Better at sustaining blood levels

üëÅÔ∏è Eyesight & Myopia

The myopia epidemic is the strongest evidence that environment trumps genetics in modern disease.

Common Belief

"Bad eyesight is genetic" HIGH confidence it's wrong

If myopia were primarily genetic:

  • Rates would be stable across generations. Instead: 10-15% ‚Üí 40-50% in Western countries in one generation
  • East Asia: 80-90% of young adults are myopic (up from 20-30% in grandparents' generation)
  • Indigenous populations worldwide have near-zero myopia ‚Äî until they adopt modern lifestyles
  • DNA doesn't mutate in 30 years. This is an environmental epidemic, not a genetic one.
Actual Causes

Environment > Genetics

  • Lack of outdoor time / sunlight ‚Äî bright light triggers retinal dopamine release, which inhibits axial elongation of the eyeball (the physical change that causes myopia). 2+ hours/day outdoors dramatically reduces risk.
  • Excessive near-work ‚Äî screens, books, phones at 20-30cm signal the eye to elongate for near focus (accommodative lag theory)
  • Nutritional deficiency ‚Äî DHA (retina is 50% DHA), vitamin A (rhodopsin synthesis), zinc (retina metabolism)
  • Peripheral defocus ‚Äî corrective lenses that don't address peripheral defocus may accelerate progression
Key Studies

The Evidence Is Overwhelming

StudyFinding
Sydney Myopia Study (2008)Children spending >2h/day outdoors had 1/4 the myopia rate, regardless of near-work hours. Outdoor time was the strongest predictor ‚Äî even in genetically predisposed children.

Opposing View

"Myopia is largely heritable" ‚Äî GWAS studies

Genome-wide association studies have identified 160+ genetic loci associated with myopia. Twin studies suggest 60-80% heritability. Why this is misleading:

  • Heritability ‚â† destiny. Heritability measures variance within a population under current conditions. If everyone shares the same harmful environment, genetic differences explain the remaining variation ‚Äî but the ENVIRONMENT is still the primary cause.
  • Analogy: In a population where everyone smokes, lung cancer would appear "highly heritable" because genetic differences in detox enzymes would explain who gets it. That doesn't mean smoking isn't the cause.
  • The timeline disproves pure genetics. 400% increase in one generation = environment. Genetics loads the susceptibility; modern lifestyle pulls the trigger.
‚ö° Genetics loads the gun, environment pulls the trigger. Some people are more genetically susceptible. But the explosive increase in myopia is 100% environmental ‚Äî lack of outdoor time, excessive near-work, and nutritional deficiency. Get outside, eat DHA, take breaks from screens, and give your eyes distance viewing time.

ü•õ Milk & Growth

Milk is a growth fluid designed by evolution. The question isn't whether it works ‚Äî it's how to use it optimally.

IGF-1 Mechanism HIGH confidence

Milk doesn't contain meaningful growth hormone (bovine GH is species-specific and destroyed by stomach acid). What it does:

  • Stimulates endogenous IGF-1 production ‚Äî milk's amino acid profile (particularly BCAAs and whey protein) signals the liver to produce more IGF-1
  • IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1) ‚Äî the primary mediator of growth hormone's effects on bone and tissue growth
  • Each additional cup of milk ‚Üí approximately +0.4cm average height increase (observational data)
  • Effect is synergistic with adequate sleep (GH peaks 1-2h after sleep onset) and exercise
Hoppe et al. (2006). "Cow's milk and linear growth in industrialized and developing countries." Annu Rev Nutr. doi:10.1146/annurev.nutr.26.010506.103757

Growth Hormone & Sleep HIGH confidence

  • GH secretion pattern: largest pulse occurs 1-2 hours after sleep onset, during deep (N3) sleep
  • Fasting enhances GH secretion ‚Äî insulin suppresses it
  • Exercise (especially high-intensity/resistance) acutely increases GH
  • Optimal timing: Drink milk 2-3 hours before bed ‚Üí amino acids available for IGF-1 ‚Üí GH surge during sleep ‚Üí maximum growth stimulus

Why Gym Performance Drops Initially HIGH confidence

  • Glycolytic pathways downregulated ‚Äî heavy lifting and sprints rely on fast glycolysis. Without glycogen reserves and with pyruvate dehydrogenase kinase upregulated, your anaerobic power drops 10-30%.
  • Creatine phosphate unaffected ‚Äî very short efforts (1-5 sec) are fine. It's the 15-60 second range that suffers most.
  • Solution: Drop weights 20-30% during adaptation. Focus on volume over intensity. 2-3x/week is plenty. Don't chase PRs for 4-6 weeks.
  • After adaptation: Aerobic performance may exceed pre-keto levels. Anaerobic may remain slightly lower but compensated by improved recovery and endurance.

The Randle Cycle

Fat + Carbs Together = Problem HIGH confidence

The Randle cycle (glucose-fatty acid cycle) describes metabolic competition between glucose and fat oxidation:

  • When both glucose and fatty acids are available, cells must choose which to burn
  • High glucose ‚Üí insulin ‚Üí suppresses fat oxidation ‚Üí fat gets stored
  • High fat + high carb simultaneously ‚Üí insulin resistance pathway (muscles reject glucose in favor of fat, glucose stays elevated, more insulin needed)
  • This is why high-fat + high-carb is the worst combination ‚Äî it's not fat OR carbs that cause metabolic syndrome, it's both together
  • Pick a lane: high-fat/low-carb (keto) OR high-carb/low-fat. The SAD combines the worst of both.
Randle et al. (1963). "The glucose fatty-acid cycle: its role in insulin sensitivity and the metabolic disturbances of diabetes mellitus." Lancet. doi:10.1016/S0140-6736(63)91500-9
üî¨ Honest Take: Keto adaptation physiology is well-characterized. The timeline is consistent across studies and anecdotal reports. The Randle cycle is textbook biochemistry. Where it gets debatable: long-term keto vs cyclical keto vs carnivore, optimal protein levels on keto (too much protein ‚Üí gluconeogenesis vs adequate for muscle preservation), and whether everyone responds equally well. Some people thrive on keto for years; others do better cycling carbs around exercise. Individual variation is real. The 4-week commitment is non-negotiable for any fair test.

üí• Myth Busters

The biggest lies in nutrition, held up to the light. Each myth includes the evidence for, against, and an honest assessment.

Myth

"Saturated fat causes heart disease" HIGH confidence it's wrong

Based on Ancel Keys' Seven Countries Study (1958) ‚Äî cherry-picked 7 of 22 available countries to show a correlation. When all 22 are plotted, the correlation disappears.

  • Siri-Tarino et al. (2010) meta-analysis of 21 prospective studies, 347,747 subjects: "No significant evidence that saturated fat is associated with increased risk of CHD or CVD." doi:10.3945/ajcn.2009.27725
  • Chowdhury et al. (2014) meta-analysis, 72 studies, 600,000+ participants: No association between saturated fat and cardiovascular disease. doi:10.7326/M13-1788
  • Harcombe et al. (2015) re-examined the original data behind dietary guidelines: "Dietary recommendations were introduced for 220 million US and 56 million UK citizens by 1983, in the absence of supporting evidence." doi:10.1136/openhrt-2014-000196

What actually matters: Oxidized LDL (from seed oils), systemic inflammation, insulin resistance, and metabolic syndrome ‚Äî not saturated fat intake.

Myth

"Dietary cholesterol raises blood cholesterol" HIGH confidence it's wrong

Your liver produces ~80% of your cholesterol. When you eat more, your liver makes less (feedback regulation). The 2015-2020 Dietary Guidelines finally removed the cholesterol limit, stating "cholesterol is not a nutrient of concern for overconsumption."

  • Eggs contain ~186mg cholesterol each. Egg consumption studies show negligible impact on blood cholesterol in most people.
  • ~25% of people are "hyper-responders" who see LDL increase ‚Äî but their LDL particles tend to be large/buoyant (benign pattern A), not small/dense (atherogenic pattern B).
Myth

"Seed oils are heart-healthy" HIGH confidence it's wrong

Canola, soybean, sunflower, corn oil ‚Äî 50-75% PUFA (mostly linoleic acid), extracted with hexane solvent, degummed, bleached, and deodorized at 200-270¬∞C. They're oxidized before you buy them.

  • The Sydney Diet Heart Study (1966-73): replacing saturated fat with safflower oil (LA) increased all-cause mortality by 62% and cardiovascular mortality by 70%. doi:10.1136/bmj.e8707 (re-analyzed 2013)
  • The Minnesota Coronary Experiment (1968-73): corn oil replacement lowered cholesterol but increased mortality. For every 30mg/dL cholesterol lowering, there was a 22% higher risk of death. doi:10.1136/bmj.i1246 (re-analyzed 2016)
  • Modern seed oil consumption is ~80g/day in the US ‚Äî this is an evolutionary novelty. Our ancestors consumed 1-4g/day of LA.
Myth

"Sunscreen prevents cancer ‚Äî always wear it" MEDIUM confidence it's misleading

Melanoma rates have increased despite sunscreen use increasing. The relationship is more nuanced:

  • Most sunscreens block UVB (which triggers vitamin D synthesis) but allow UVA through (which penetrates deeper and may cause more DNA damage)
  • Vitamin D deficiency (from sun avoidance + sunscreen) is associated with higher rates of colorectal, breast, prostate cancer, MS, heart disease, depression
  • A 2016 Swedish study (Lindqvist et al.) of 29,518 women over 20 years: women who avoided sun exposure had 2x higher all-cause mortality than highest sun exposure group. doi:10.1111/joim.12496
  • Nuance: Burning is genuinely damaging (DNA repair overwhelmed). Regular, moderate sun exposure without burning is protective. The dose makes the difference.

They give you ALA ‚Äî a precursor that converts to the usable forms (EPA/DHA) at 5-10% and 1-3% respectively. That's like saying you have a car because you own 3% of a tire. Eat fish.

Myopia rates exploded 400-500% in one generation. DNA doesn't mutate that fast. Indigenous populations prove it ‚Äî near-zero myopia until modern lifestyle adoption. Outdoor time, not genes, is the primary driver. (See Chapter 7 for full evidence.)

üî¨ Epistemic Honesty

How to think about nutrition science ‚Äî its strengths, weaknesses, and why you should question everything, including this guide.

How to Read Nutrition Studies

  • Check the study type: RCT > prospective cohort > retrospective cohort > case-control > case report > expert opinion
  • Check the funding: Industry-funded studies are 4-8x more likely to report favorable outcomes for the funder's product (Lexchin et al. 2003)
  • Check the endpoints: "LDL went down" is a surrogate endpoint. "People lived longer" is a hard endpoint. Surrogate endpoints have been wrong many times (see: margarine lowered cholesterol but increased mortality).
  • Check the duration: Nutrition effects often take years to manifest. A 12-week study can miss chronic accumulation effects (AGEs, seed oil tissue loading, etc.)
  • Check the confounders: Did they control for exercise, sleep, smoking, alcohol, socioeconomic status? Usually not all of them.

Major Biases in Nutrition Research

BiasWhat It IsExample
Healthy User BiasPeople who follow health recommendations also exercise, sleep better, smoke less, etc. The behavior is confounded with overall health consciousness."Whole grain eaters live longer" ‚Äî they also exercise more, drink less, and have higher income.
Industry FundingCompanies fund studies designed to find results favorable to their products.Coca-Cola funded "exercise not diet" research. Sugar industry paid Harvard scientists to blame fat (1960s). Seed oil companies fund LA safety studies.
Publication BiasPositive results get published; null results don't.For every study showing a supplement works, there may be 5 unpublished studies showing it doesn't.
Recall BiasPeople poorly remember what they ate. Food frequency questionnaires are notoriously inaccurate.Virtually all observational nutrition studies rely on self-reported dietary recall.
Surrogate Endpoint TrapMeasuring a biomarker (LDL, blood pressure) instead of actual outcomes (death, disease).Multiple drugs lowered LDL but increased mortality (torcetrapib, CETP inhibitors).

Why RCTs Are Hard in Nutrition

  • Blinding is impossible ‚Äî you can't give someone a placebo steak. People know what they're eating.
  • Compliance is poor ‚Äî keeping people on a strict diet for years is unrealistic outside of metabolic ward studies
  • Duration needed is long ‚Äî chronic disease takes decades. Most RCTs run 6 months to 2 years.
  • Ethical constraints ‚Äî you can't randomize children to nutritionally deficient diets to study growth
  • Cost ‚Äî large, long-duration RCTs cost hundreds of millions. Who pays? Usually industry (see bias above).
  • Result: We're stuck with mostly observational data, supplemented by mechanistic studies and small metabolic ward trials. This is why nutrition science is perpetually uncertain.

Our Approach

Given the limitations above, we use a triangulation method:

  • 1. Mechanistic reasoning ‚Äî does the biochemistry make sense? (e.g., lipid peroxidation chemistry is well-characterized)
  • 2. Ancestral observation ‚Äî what did humans eat for 200,000+ years before chronic disease became epidemic? (Answer: meat, organs, fish, some plants, zero seed oils)
  • 3. Best available evidence ‚Äî meta-analyses, RCTs where they exist, cohort studies with proper controls
  • 4. N=1 experimentation ‚Äî your body is your lab. Track what you eat, how you feel, your biomarkers. Adjust.

When all four converge, confidence is high. When they conflict, we flag uncertainty. We don't claim certainty we don't have.

The Replication Crisis

A 2015 study in Science attempted to replicate 100 psychology studies. Only 36% replicated. Nutrition science is likely similar or worse. Many "landmark" findings that shaped dietary guidelines were never replicated or were later contradicted.

  • The original dietary cholesterol limit? Based on rabbit studies (herbivores, completely different lipid metabolism)
  • The "low-fat is healthy" consensus? Based on the flawed Seven Countries Study and confirmed by studies now shown to be industry-influenced
  • The food pyramid? Created by USDA ‚Äî an agency whose mandate is to promote American agriculture, not optimize human health
Open Science Collaboration (2015). "Estimating the reproducibility of psychological science." Science. doi:10.1126/science.aac4716
